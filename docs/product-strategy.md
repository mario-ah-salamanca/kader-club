# Product Strategy

## Executive Summary

Kader Club should become the trusted system of record for European football-card collectors before it becomes a marketplace.

The product combines the practical utility of a portfolio tracker with the emotional feeling of a digital club archive.

The current product foundation is:

- Digital Binder
- Portfolio Dashboard
- Curated football-card catalog
- Wishlist
- Trade discovery

## Product Modes

Kader Club should balance two modes:

### Portfolio Brain

- Valuation
- Profit/loss
- Confidence labels
- Wishlist
- Trade matching
- Market signals

### Collector Heart

- Binder view
- Favorite cards
- Club identity
- Set completion
- Personal showcase
- Future Fantasy XI squad-building

## Positioning

Primary positioning:

> Kader Club is the Transfermarkt for football cards — a portfolio tracker, digital binder, and collector network for football-card collectors.

Near-term product identity:

> A premium digital binder and portfolio dashboard.

Long-term product identity:

> The collector operating system for football cards.

## Non-Negotiable Product Principles

1. The collection is the foundation.
2. Catalog quality is a product moat.
3. Price estimates must be transparent.
4. Trading should begin as discovery, not transaction execution.
5. The product must not remove the fun of cards.
6. Complexity should be progressively disclosed.

## User Segments

### Casual Trader

Mindset:

> I want to organize my cards, see what I have, and maybe trade occasionally.

Primary jobs:

- Add cards without spreadsheets.
- See simple estimated value.
- Showcase favorite cards.
- Mark duplicates or unwanted cards as open to trade.

Design requirement:

- Keep the experience visual, lightweight, and low-pressure.

### Hobby Trader

Mindset:

> I want to improve my collection through smart trades while staying connected to the hobby.

Primary jobs:

- Build a wishlist.
- Find people who own cards I want.
- Compare trade value fairly.
- Track set/player/club goals.

Design requirement:

- Show trade context, wishlist matches, and fairness cues without overwhelming the user.

### Power Trader

Mindset:

> I want portfolio control, price intelligence, alerts, and efficient deal flow.

Primary jobs:

- Track cost basis and ROI.
- Monitor price movement.
- Use bulk tools.
- Scan for high-quality trade opportunities.
- Analyze exposure by player, club, set, season, and grade.

Design requirement:

- Allow dense data, advanced filters, and faster workflows without intimidating casual users.

## Core User Loop

```txt
Sign up
→ Create profile
→ Add cards
→ See portfolio value
→ Mark trade availability
→ Add wishlist items
→ Receive trade matches
→ Send trade interest
→ Update collection
→ New values and matches appear
```

## Product North Star

Weekly active collectors who update their collection, wishlist, or trade status.
